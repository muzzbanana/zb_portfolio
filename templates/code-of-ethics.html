My Code of Ethics

1. Accessibility

As a designer, I will do my best to make sure that all of my designs are available and accessible to all users.

2. Picking Ethical Projects

As a designer, I reserve the right to decline any projects that I believe contradict my moral or ethical codes.

3. Accepting My Limitations

As a human and a designer, I believe it is important to remember that my experience is limited to my point of view. There are thousands of unique perspectives and it is important to keep an open mind and seek out varying viewpoints throughout the design process.

4. Protecting Data

As a designer, I will always work hard to safeguard my user’s data. Moreover, I will always be honest and upfront about when and how data is being used.

5. Respectful Criticism

When my work is critiqued, I will not take criticisms personally and I will always listen to others’ ideas on how I can improve.

When critiquing other designers work, I will always be respectful of the person and the effort that they put into their projects. I will not be intentionally cruel and I will always include an explanation to back up any critique I make.
