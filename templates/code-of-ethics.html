My Code of Ethics

1. Accessibility

As a designer, I will do my best to make sure that all of my designs are available and accessible to all users, even if that comes at a cost to my own designs. If I have to choose between a more polished design or a more accessible design, I will always choose the accessible option.

2. Picking Ethical Projects

As a designer, I reserve the right to decline any projects that I believe contradict my moral or ethical codes. I will not work on projects that I believe will have an obvious negative impact on my community or the greater world.

I will not work on projects that are insidiously habit forming, environmentally damaging, or predatory against vulnerable users.

3. Accepting My Limitations

As a human and a designer, I believe it is important to remember that my experience is limited to my point of view. There are thousands of unique perspectives out in the world and it is important to keep this at the forefront of my mind as I design and create solutions.

4. Designing for Good

As a human, I know that I am flawed and incapable of predicting every possible outcome. I will do my best to make sure my designs are not evil, but if I accidentally create a design that has negative consequences on other people’s lives, I will listen and learn from my mistakes. I will apologize and correct the behavior as best I can, but I will do so with the understanding that these unintended consequences were not intentional.

5. Protecting Data

As a designer, I will always work hard to safeguard my user’s data and I will always be honest and upfront about when and how it is being used. I will never sell a user’s data or give it away without their explicit consent.

6. Respectful Criticism

When critiquing other designers work, I will always be respectful of the person and the effort that they put into their projects. I will not be intentionally cruel and I will always include an explanation to back up any critique I make.

When being critiqued, I will not take criticisms personally and I will listen to others’ ideas on how I can improve my work.
