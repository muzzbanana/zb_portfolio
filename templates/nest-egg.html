Overview

Summary

Nest Egg is a real estate focused responsive website that I designed to help real estate investors find the perfect property. The app is designed to help users find homes, commercial buildings, or lots all around the world and give them an insightful projection on the property’s current and future value. It is designed to help people find and grow their “nest egg.”

My role

Nest Egg was an independent project. I was responsible for conducting research, strategizing the UX and designing the final UI.

Timeline and Tools

December 2019 - April 20202
Paper-pencil, Sketch, Adobe XD, Adobe Illustrator

Project Brief

Real estate investment is an increasingly popular and exciting way for individuals to achieve financial security and diversify their portfolios. It is an exciting and emotional experience, but often complicated. While there are plenty of websites, blogs, and agencies providing information, often, buyers new to the market may struggle to get started without professional guidance and waste time viewing properties out of their range. The aim of this project will be to create a website that helps users find the perfect property in a crowded market, regardless of their experience level with real estate.

Approach

Nest Egg was designed with a user-centric mobile first approach, based on heuristic research and validated assumptions.

Introduction

The project began with creating a site map and rapidly sketching wireframes. This process allowed me to quickly test multiple ideas and decide which direction I wanted to go in. The big choice here was to use a tab to display the high priority information on each property, which would allow users to quickly get an overview of a house without leaving the main page.

Refining the Design

Once I had finished with the base designs for all of my screens, I began to expand the designs out to meet larger break proints. During this process, I was able to review the pages and make new iterative changes that improved the aesthetics and usability of the design as a whole. This involved making considerable changes to the Listing page, creating a pixel perfect logo, increasing the size of buttons, tweaking colors, and many other small changes that created huge improvements in the user interface.

Home Page Updates

  • I added a blue outline to the price style to increase its readability.
  • I increased the size of the buttons to improve usability.
  • I used Adobe Illustrator to create custom pixel-perfect icons and a pixel-perfect logo.

Listing Page Changes

  • I broke up the page to use a table of contents, rather than asking users to scroll to the end of the page. This will help users navigate to the sections they want more easily and quickly.
  • The new page design allows the CTA's to remain ever-present on the page, which will increase the likelihood users complete the tasks.
  • I changed the photo carousel so that it is easier for users to see where they are in the slideshow.
  • I increased the white space between the photos and the information to make the page look less busy and more refined.
